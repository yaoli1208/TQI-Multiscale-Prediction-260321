**Contribution to Intelligent Transportation Systems**

Railway track maintenance is a critical component of intelligent transportation infrastructure. While significant advances have been made in vehicle-side intelligence (autonomous driving, V2X), infrastructure-side intelligence—particularly predictive maintenance based on track condition monitoring—remains underexplored. This paper addresses this gap by proposing a novel framework that bridges data-driven machine learning with domain expertise from railway maintenance practice.

**Key Contributions and Innovation**

The primary innovation of this work is the explicit encoding of business knowledge (maintenance cycles, seasonal patterns, degradation trends) into a multi-scale prediction framework. Unlike black-box deep learning approaches that require massive datasets, our Trident framework:

1. **Requires minimal data**: Effective with only hundreds of records, enabling deployment in data-scarce scenarios
2. **Is fully interpretable**: Predictions are based on maintenance anchors, seasonal adjustments, and degradation trends—concepts familiar to field engineers
3. **Handles maintenance interventions**: Explicitly models the discontinuities caused by major repairs, a challenge that traditional statistical methods fail to address
4. **Aligns with engineering workflows**: The "post-maintenance prediction" strategy directly supports the "observe-after-repair" practice used by railway maintenance departments

**Technical Significance**

Our experiments on two real-world high-speed railway sections demonstrate MAE reductions of 26%-78% compared to traditional statistical methods. Notably, on the complex volatile section (Sample 3), our method achieves MAE=0.294 while exponential smoothing fails catastrophically (MAE=1.355). This robustness to real-world complexity is crucial for practical deployment.
